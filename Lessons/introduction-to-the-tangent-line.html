Introduction to the Tangent Line

If you've taken geometry and looked at circles before, you probably have heard of the term tangent. Or maybe you've heard of the phrase to "go on a tangent". A tangent is something that touches but does not cross over a line. Below is an example of a tangent of a circle. When we talk about tangents to a function, we mean a straight line that just barely touches, but does not intersect, a graph at one point. For example, look at the following graph: $$f(x)=x^2$$ You may have noticed that the x-axis, in fact, is tangent to the parabola above. But how do we find a tangent line to a function? And how do we know the slope of the line at the tangent?

To find a tangent line, we make a similar type of line called a secant line. A secant line is another, less common word used in geometry. Secant lines, unlike tangent lines, intersect graphs as shown below. The graph of \(f(x)=x^{2}\) with a secant line is shown here: You may recall that the slope between two points is rise over run, or $$m=\frac{rise}{run}=\frac{Δy}{Δx}=\frac{y_{2}-y_{1}}{x_{2}-x_{1}}$$ In this graph, since the secant line goes through \((0,0)\) and \((1,1)\), the slope is $$m=\frac{1-0}{1-0}=1$$ Try using the interactive graph below to make a secant line through different points on the graph. To make this secant line, which goes through two points, become a tangent line, which goes through one point, we must bring both points as close to each other as possible. For example, the points \((0.9, 0.81)\) and \((1, 1)\) are really close and the secant line between them have a slope of $$\frac{1-0.81}{1-0.9}=\frac{0.19}{0.1}=1.9$$ To solve this, we use the following limit: $$\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$ All you need to do is replace f(x) with the actual function, and then you just solve the limit. If you don't remember how to do this, view our lessons on limits here. At the point \((1,1)\), the limit is $$\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{(x+h)^2-(x)^2}{h}=\frac{2xh+h^2}{h}=2x$$ At \(x=1\), the slope is 2. Using the point slope form of a linear equation, the tangent line is $$y-1=2(x-1)$$
